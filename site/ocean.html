World 01 · Ocean

The blue we came from.

Reefs, marine reserves, and the fish that remember where to spawn — the science, the divers, and a coast worth defending.

The sea raised me. My work here asks a simple, urgent question: when we protect a piece of ocean, does life really come back — and what does it take to make protection real?

The work

What I do in the ocean

your MPA photo
Research · MPAs

The edges of protection

My research revealed "edge effects" around marine protected areas — fishing pressure that reaches inside their borders. Published in Nature Ecology & Evolution and Fish & Fisheries, and covered worldwide.

your dive-survey photo
Community · Citizen science

Sea Guard

A movement of recreational divers I founded — trained to survey marine life and litter, remove ghost nets, and stand watch over the coast. Science in the hands of the people who love the sea.

your Bat-Galim photo
Conservation · Bat-Galim

A reef worth a reserve

Leading the ecological survey of the Bat-Galim rocky reef and building the case — with the community — for a new marine protected area. Watch the films, meet the reef.

your herring photo
Research · Pacific herring

The memory of a fish

In British Columbia, I study how herring carry a spatial "memory" of where to spawn — and how losing their oldest fish collapses that memory, and the places they return to.
