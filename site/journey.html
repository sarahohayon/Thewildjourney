My Journey

Everyone can change their life —
and the world around them.

This is the belief my whole path is built on. Here's where it came from.

I've never believed nature and people are two separate causes. My life's work has been to weave them together — because when a person falls for the wild, they find their own power to protect it.

It began on the water. Before I was a scientist, I taught high-risk teenagers to sail, and guided outdoor journeys for people the world had written off — watching the sea build confidence, discipline, and belief where none had been. That lesson never left me: nature changes people.

Then I went deep into how nature works — a B.Sc. in marine sciences, an M.Sc. in microbial ecology, and a PhD studying how life spills across the borders of marine protected areas. My research revealed that even our best-protected seas leak at the edges, and it reached readers around the world. But data alone doesn't save a coast.

So I kept building bridges. I founded Sea Guard, turning recreational divers into a citizen-science force. I led a national program to save vanishing wetlands, and brought the business sector and local communities together to restore grasslands. I helped the Israeli Navy become guardians of the sea. And in Bat-Galim, I helped a neighbourhood become defenders of their own reef.

Today I study the memory of Pacific herring on Canada's Pacific coast — how a fish remembers where to spawn, and how we erase that memory when we take its elders. It's the same story I've followed my whole life: what we forget, we lose; what we love, we protect.

This platform is my way of handing that on. Not to be read — to be lived. To help you build your own relationship with the wild, and discover, as I did, that you can change things.
